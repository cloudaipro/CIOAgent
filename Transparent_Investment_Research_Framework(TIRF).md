# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

# Transparent Investment Research Framework (TIRF)

## Version 1.0

### Institutional Auditability & Explainability Layer for AI Investment Agents

---

# 1. Executive Summary

## Product Name

**Transparent Investment Research Framework (TIRF)**

---

## Purpose

TIRF is a framework designed to ensure that every investment recommendation generated by an AI investment system is:

* Explainable
* Traceable
* Auditable
* Reproducible
* Challengeable

TIRF introduces a mandatory research documentation layer between specialist investment agents and the investment committee.

Instead of allowing agents to output only conclusions:

```yaml
recommendation: BUY
confidence: 82
```

every conclusion must be backed by:

* Evidence
* Assumptions
* Methodology
* Sources
* Reasoning
* Counterarguments

---

# 2. Problem Statement

## Current AI Problem

Most AI investment systems produce outputs such as:

```yaml
rating: BUY
target_price: 180
confidence: 85
```

but cannot answer:

```text
Why?

What evidence supports this?

What assumptions were used?

Which source was most influential?

What would invalidate this conclusion?

Would the same inputs produce the same output?
```

This creates:

* Black-box decisions
* Low trust
* No audit trail
* No post-mortem capability

---

# 3. Vision

Every investment conclusion should satisfy:

```text
Traceable
Explainable
Reproducible
Auditable
Debatable
```

---

# 4. Design Principles

## Principle 1

Evidence Before Opinion

Agents must gather evidence before making recommendations.

---

## Principle 2

Assumptions Must Be Explicit

Hidden assumptions are prohibited.

---

## Principle 3

Reasoning Must Be Inspectable

Every recommendation must expose its rationale.

---

## Principle 4

Alternative Views Must Be Preserved

Bull and Bear arguments must be documented.

---

## Principle 5

All Conclusions Are Versioned

Historical decisions must be reviewable.

---

# 6. Mandatory Deliverables

Every specialist agent must generate:

---

## Deliverable 1

### Structured Output

Machine-readable summary.

Example:

```yaml
recommendation: BUY
confidence: 82
fair_value: 180
```

---

## Deliverable 2

### Evidence Package

Document supporting facts.

---

Schema:

```yaml
evidence:

  - source:
      SEC 10-Q

    date:
      2026-05-01

    finding:
      HBM revenue increased 120%

    impact:
      positive

    confidence:
      high
```

---

Requirements:

Minimum:

```text
3 evidence items
```

Maximum:

Unlimited

---

## Deliverable 3

### Assumption Package

Document assumptions used.

---

Schema:

```yaml
assumptions:

  revenue_growth:
    15%

  terminal_growth:
    3%

  discount_rate:
    9%

  AI_capex_growth:
    20%
```

---

Requirements:

All assumptions must be explicit.

No hidden assumptions allowed.

---

## Deliverable 4

### Reasoning Package

Explain how evidence led to conclusions.

---

Schema:

```yaml
reasoning:

  - Evidence A increases earnings outlook

  - Higher earnings imply higher valuation

  - Current price remains below fair value

conclusion:

  BUY
```

---

Requirements:

Must form a logical chain.

---

## Deliverable 5

### Counterargument Package

Document opposing viewpoints.

---

Schema:

```yaml
counterarguments:

  - HBM supply may increase

  - AI demand may slow

  - Export restrictions may tighten
```

---

Requirements:

Minimum:

```text
3 counterarguments
```

---

## Deliverable 6

### Source Package

Document all references.

---

Schema:

```yaml
sources:

  - SEC Filing

  - Earnings Call

  - Industry Report

  - Government Data
```
